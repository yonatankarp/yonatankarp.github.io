Map logic

Two separate trail systems

Ponta de Sao Lourenco is not part of the Pico do Arieiro / Pico Ruivo trail. Treat these as competing nature days, not one continuous route.

East coast peninsula

PR8 Ponta de Sao Lourenco

This is the dry exposed coastal hike at Madeira’s eastern tip. It starts and ends around Baia d’Abra, so it works cleanly by public bus.

1
FunchalStart from the city bus network.
2
SIGA route 702 to Baia d’AbraPublic transport to the PR8 trailhead.
3
Walk PR8 out and backBaia d’Abra -> Ponta de Sao Lourenco area -> return to Baia d’Abra.
4
Return route 702 to FunchalSame trailhead, same transport corridor.
Use this when you want simpler logistics. It is exposed to sun and wind but does not need a transfer pickup.

Central mountains

Pico do Arieiro to Pico Ruivo

This is the mountain ridge route. PR1 links Pico do Arieiro to Pico Ruivo; PR1.2 exits from Pico Ruivo to Achada do Teixeira.

1
Funchal to Pico do ArieiroPublic bus can work for viewpoint-only. For the full hike, a transfer is cleaner.
2
Walk PR1 to Pico RuivoOnly if the official route status allows the full traverse.
3
Exit via PR1.2 to Achada do TeixeiraThis avoids walking back across the ridge.
4
Prebook pickup / shuttleAchada do Teixeira is the weak public-transport point.
Do not plan the full Arieiro -> Ruivo -> Achada route by public transport alone. It starts and ends in different places. Also recheck the official PR1 page before booking: on 8 Jul 2026 it still flagged access only as far as Pedra Rija.

Decision rules

How to use the plan

Keep fixed

Flights and 16 Jul tour

Move the tour only if cancellation rules and weather make the switch obvious. The flight morning should stay protected.

Moveable

PR8 and Curral

Swap PR8 and Curral based on wind, heat, and cloud. PR8 is exposed; Curral is the safer fallback.

Avoid duplicates

Do not re-do the west

If the tour scratches the Cabo Girao / west coast itch, spend 18 July in Funchal instead of repeating the same corridor.
