# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Experienced engineers, hiring managers, technical leaders, and thoughtful collaborators who want a fast read on Yonatan's judgment, range, writing, and taste. They usually arrive with limited time, often from GitHub, LinkedIn, or a shared article, and they need proof of depth without marketing fluff.

## Product Purpose
Present Yonatan as a high-trust staff-level engineer whose work spans architecture, execution, writing, and open source. The site should help visitors understand how he thinks, what he has built, and why he is worth talking to, hiring, following, or collaborating with. Success looks like a site that feels precise, calm, and unmistakably personal rather than template-driven.

## Brand Personality
Careful, technical, human.

Voice should feel expert and grounded, with quiet confidence instead of self-promotion. The emotional goal is trust first, curiosity second.

## Anti-references
Do not look like a generic SaaS template, a bootcamp portfolio, or a dark-mode hacker cliché. Avoid startup gradient gloss, feature-card spam, fake metrics, loud self-branding, or “AI-made landing page” symmetry. Do not frame Yonatan as a product mascot or thought-leader caricature.

## Design Principles
1. Show engineering judgment, not personal branding theater.
2. Let structure do the persuasion, visitors should discover credibility through substance.
3. Keep the interface warm and literate, but still technically sharp.
4. Treat writing, projects, and experience as one coherent body of work.
5. Bilingual support is a first-class part of the product, not a bolt-on translation mode.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.2 AA contrast and keyboard accessibility. Respect reduced motion preferences. Preserve comfortable reading widths and strong focus states. Ensure Hebrew layouts feel native in RTL, not mirrored after the fact.
